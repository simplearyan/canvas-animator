The Sleep Economy

Tracking the $500B boom in rest-based technology and the biometrics of the bedroom.

Live Metric // Rest Cycle Pulse

Humanity has a complicated relationship with the pillow. For millennia, sleep was the ultimate utility—a biological necessity that cost nothing and required no equipment.

In the last decade, rest has been rebranded as a performance metric. We no longer just "sleep"; we optimize our circadian rhythms using a massive industrial complex of hardware.

The Gamification of Rest

We are seeing a shift toward 'orthosomnia'—an anxiety-induced insomnia caused by the obsession with perfect sleep metrics. People are competing for high scores on their biometric rings, ironically sacrificing the very thing they aim to improve.
